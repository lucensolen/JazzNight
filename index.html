The Room

What you step into when the door closes behind you.

This isn’t “live music in a bar.” It’s a room built around one night of jazz: tuned sound, controlled lighting, and a layout that makes every booth feel like the best seat in the house.

Red Velvet & Brass

Deep red booths, dark walnut tables, brass fittings and low, cinematic light. No harsh LEDs, no blue wash. Just warmth and shadow.

  • Booth layout designed as a crescent around the band.
  • Each table has its own brass lamp and service bell.
  • Every angle looks like a still from a film.

Sound Tuned for Sax

The room isn’t loud for the sake of it. It’s tuned so the sax cuts through smooth, the upright bass sits underneath, and you can still talk between sets.

  • Front-of-house levels managed all night.
  • No background playlists bleeding into the set.
  • Every seat is in the sound field, not behind it.

Set Flow & Story

Three sets, each with a different mood: arrival, burn, and afterglow. You’re not just attending a gig—you’re in a plotted evening.

  • Arrival set: standards with space to breathe.
  • Middle set: darker, heavier, more cinematic.
  • Final set: low, late, and almost private.

Set Flow

The night in three movements.

Set I · ARRIVAL

Doors, first pour, first notes.

Guests arrive into low light and soft standards. Enough space to recognise each other, order a drink, and feel the room exhale.

  • Doors 7:30 PM · Set 8:15–9:00 PM
  • Opening standards, cinematic but gentle.
  • Perfect time for photos and introductions.

Set II · BURN

Lights down, tempo up.

This is where the room leans in. Darker tunes, heavier bass, brass cutting straight through the red velvet and shadow.

  • Set 9:30–10:15 PM
  • Feature solos and full-band improvisations.
  • Room runs hotter, conversations fall away.

Set III · AFTERGLOW

The room goes late and low.

Final set feels like a secret. Slower, moodier pieces. The kind of last notes you still hear in your head on the walk home.

  • Set 10:45–11:30 PM
  • Closer tunes and encore if the room calls for it.
  • Bar stays open, lights stay low.

Details

Questions people ask before stepping through the door.

Is this a recurring night?

No. This exact room, this layout, this build is for one night only. Future nights may echo it, but nothing repeats exactly.

Is there food?

Light dishes and bar snacks only. This is a jazz room with a kitchen, not the other way around.

Can I come alone?

Yes. The Brass Rail is perfect for solos and pairs. You’ll still feel part of the room, not left on the edge.

What if I need to cancel?

Booth cancellations within 72 hours can be moved to a future build. Rail seats are non-refundable but transferable.
